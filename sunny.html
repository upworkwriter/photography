Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Sunny 16 rule in film photography?
The Sunny 16 rule is a guideline that suggests setting your aperture to f/16 and your shutter speed to the reciprocal of your films ISO on a sunny day for correct exposure.
Why is the Sunny 16 rule useful for photographers?
It provides a simple way to estimate proper exposure without using a light meter, especially helpful in bright daylight conditions.
How do you adjust settings using the Sunny 16 rule if lighting conditions change from sunny to cloudy?
For partly cloudy conditions, open up one stop (e.g., f/11); for overcast days, open up two stops (e.g., f/8).
Can the Sunny 16 rule be applied with different ISO films?
Yes, adjust the shutter speed according to your film’s ISO. For example, with ISO 100 film, use a shutter speed of 1/100 second at f/16 on a sunny day.
